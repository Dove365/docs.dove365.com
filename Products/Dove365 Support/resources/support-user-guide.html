Daily Support Workflow

A practical day-to-day workflow for working queues, cases, support activities, managed services, and dashboards in Dove365 Support.

Scope

Use this as the daily runbook

Use this guide when you are working support items in the Dove365 Support app. It focuses on the normal daily sequence: check dashboards, pick queue work, update Cases, log Support Activities, review Managed Service context, then resolve or hand over the work.

For detailed field-by-field guidance, use the related record guides linked at the end of this page.

Start of day

Start with your dashboard

  1. Open the Dove365 Support app.
  2. Open the My Work dashboard.
  3. Review My Queue Items to see work currently assigned to you through Queue Item Worked By.
  4. Review My Open Cases to see active Cases in your queue.
  5. Review Cases Available to Pick to understand unassigned work in the default queue.
  6. Review overdue activities before starting new work.
  7. Open high-priority or overdue Cases first.
Triage

Pick or review queue work

Use Queue Items to understand what work is available, what is already being worked, and what needs assignment.

  1. Open the relevant queue or available queue items view.
  2. Check whether the item is already being worked.
  3. Pick the item when you are taking ownership.
  4. Open the related Case before starting investigation.
Case work

Update the Case

The Case is the main support work record. Before doing detailed work, make sure the Case has the correct customer, classification, service context, owner, and priority.

  1. Confirm the Account and Contact are correct.
  2. Set Category, Subcategory, Service Offering, and Managed Service where applicable.
  3. Record investigation notes and customer updates on the Case timeline.
  4. Escalate only when another owner or manager needs to intervene.
Activity logging

Log support activity

Use Support Activities to record the work performed against a Case, including time spent and service delivery notes. This keeps the Case history complete and supports Managed Service usage rollups.

  • Log meaningful notes while the work is fresh.
  • Record time against the correct Case.
  • Use Support Activities for support delivery work, not just generic activity history.
  • Check the related Managed Service when time should affect allocation usage.
Service context

Check managed service context

When a Case is linked to a Managed Service, check the service allocation before committing more work. This gives users and account managers the context they need before hours increase.

  • Confirm the selected Managed Service is the right one for the customer and service type.
  • Review allocation, hours used, and hours remaining.
  • Flag exceptions or over-allocation risks to the account manager.
Close or hand over

Resolve, reassign, or hand over

Finish each item with a clear next state. A Case should not sit open without ownership, current notes, or a next action.

  1. Resolve the Case when the customer issue is complete.
  2. Reassign or escalate the Case if another user needs to continue it.
  3. Leave clear notes for the next person if the Case remains open.
  4. Return to My Support Work to pick the next item.
